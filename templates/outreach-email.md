# Outreach Email Templates

Copy-paste starting points for link-building and digital PR outreach. See [SEO Book, Chapter 18: Off-Page SEO & Link Building](../docs/books/complete/seo/chapter-18.md) and the [Link Building Prompts](../prompts/link-building.md) for drafting help.

**Before sending any of these:** personalize every bracketed field with something genuinely specific to the recipient — a generic mail-merge version of any of these reads as spam and damages your domain's outreach reputation for future attempts.

---

## 1. Linkable Asset Pitch

```text
Subject: [Specific detail from their article] + a resource that might help

Hi [Name],

I read your piece on [their specific article title/URL] — [one genuine,
specific reaction to something in it, not generic flattery].

I recently put together [asset name/type: a data study / free tool / guide]
on [topic], which covers [the one concrete thing relevant to their audience]:
[URL]

No obligation at all, but if it's useful context for your readers, feel free
to link to it.

Thanks for the great work on [publication/site name].

[Your name]
```

**When to use:** Pitching a genuinely useful linkable asset (data study, free tool, comprehensive guide) to a site whose existing content shows real topical overlap.

---

## 2. Unlinked Mention Follow-Up

```text
Subject: Thanks for the mention in [article title]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for mentioning [your brand/product] in [article title/URL] — glad it
was useful context for your readers.

If it's easy on your end, would you mind linking the mention to [specific
URL]? Totally understand if not, just thought I'd ask.

Thanks again,
[Your name]
```

**When to use:** Following up on an existing unlinked brand mention — keep it low-pressure, since the mention already happened without you asking.

---

## 3. Broken Link Replacement

```text
Subject: Found a broken link on [page title]

Hi [Name],

I was reading [page title/URL] and noticed the link to [broken URL] returns
a 404 — thought you'd want to know.

If you're looking for a replacement, I have a resource on the same topic
here: [your URL]. No worries either way, just wanted to flag the broken link.

[Your name]
```

**When to use:** Genuine broken-link building — only pitch a replacement that's a real, comparable fit for what the original link covered, not just any content of yours.

---

## 4. Guest Content Pitch

```text
Subject: Guest post idea for [publication name]: [proposed title]

Hi [Name],

I'd like to pitch a guest piece for [publication name]: "[proposed title]" —
covering [one-sentence summary of the unique angle, not just the topic].

A bit about me: [one sentence of genuinely relevant credibility/experience].
Recent writing sample: [URL].

Happy to adjust the angle to fit what's worked well for your readers. Let me
know if this is a fit.

[Your name]
```

**When to use:** Pitching original guest content — lead with a specific angle, not just a topic area, and back it with a real writing sample.

---

## Rules these templates follow

- Reference something specific to the recipient, never a generic opener
- State the ask clearly and once — no burying it in flattery
- Make declining easy ("no worries either way") — this is honest, not just polite; genuine no-pressure outreach performs better over repeated attempts than high-pressure asks
- Never bulk-send these unmodified — see the [Link Building Checklist](../checklists/launch-checklist.md)
